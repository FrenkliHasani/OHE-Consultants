About us


About OHE

OHE is a Canadian company with the head office located in Mississauga, Ontario, and a network of satellite offices in British Columbia, Alberta, Quebec, Nova Scotia and Peterborough (Ontario). This network of offices enables OHE to provide its national clients with efficient and consistent quality services across Canada.

OHE is a multidisciplinary firm that specializes in providing high quality services in the field of environmental, health and safety and building science consulting. OHE has provided such services to a wide base of clientele including financial institutions, property management groups, retail companies and facilities, manufacturing and pharmaceutical industries, government agencies and municipalities, health care and research facilities, insurance and legal clients.

The many consulting services offered by OHE include occupational and environmental hygiene, environmental site assessments and remediation, property condition assessments and restoration design, tendering and management, asbestos and building hazardous materials management and control, indoor air quality, building microbiological contamination (mould) management and control, environment and health and safety training and the development and implementation of environmental and health and safety management systems. Additional specialty engineering services include risk management, forensic engineering, confined space management and water/wastewater engineering, pollution prevention and auditing.

Project Specialist

OHE Consultants is looking to hire Junior to Senior Project Specialists in the area of Building Science Engineering. There are positions for Junior to Senior Project Specialists to join our expanding Building Science services delivery team. The successful candidate will be responsible for assisting to maintain effective communication and interacting with clients, contractors and OHE employees involved in the projects.

Qualifications:
1) Degree/Diploma in Civil Engineering or Technology program from accredited university or college;
2) Registered or eligible for registration as an EIT or associate member of OACETT;
3) Experience and knowledge of major building systems and building construction;
4) Minimum 0 to 3 years in the area of Building Science Engineering;
5) Proficient in using Microsoft Office (Outlook, Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Schedule);

Skills/Requirements:
1) Ability to quickly mobilize and travel mainly within Canada but also to U.S.A. and other countries is required. Typical project turn-around times can range from 5 to 15 business days. Multiple sequential sites visit requirements may require extended trips out-of-town of up to 3 weeks for efficient project delivery.
2) Possess and maintain a valid driver’s license;
3) Possess and maintain a valid passport (preferred);
4) Ability to attain police and other agency security clearance checks (CPIC, VSS, Reliability);
5) Demonstrates a passion in building science investigations, correct detailing in construction and helping building owners durably correct their building performance issues;
6) Fast learner with a desire to grow professionally and personally;
7) A team player with excellent communication skills, including verbal, written and presentation skills;
8) Inquisitive, clear thinker who can determine what work is required and who can draw rational conclusions from information available;
9) Able to conceptualize in three dimensions;
10) Strong at multi-tasking, time management and adaptable to shifting priorities;
11) Must be comfortable with ladder accessing roofs (< 10m) working safely at heights.

Responsibilities:
1) Assist with preparing bid documents including specifications, scope of work, details and drawings; Coordinate and assist project manager with building science projects;
2) Assist in preparing whole building property condition assessments for transactional due diligence following ASTM Standard Guide E 2018-08 and financial studies/life cycle costing for capital planning;
3) Assist in preparing reserve fund studies and performance audits for condominiums;
4) Draft reports prior to submitting for project manager review;
5) Coordinate with other environmental, engineering and architectural disciplines;
6) Assist in performing contract administration/construction review tasks;
7) Support existing client relationships;
8) Prepare proposals and maintain project file documentation;
9) Promote and advance OHE’s professional services by participating in training, supported by an ongoing mentoring program which focuses on skills development and employee engagement and facilitated by continuing job/project specific feedback intended to encourage continuous improvement;

The candidate must show an ability to work within a client oriented and fast-paced environment. Required skills include flexibility, adaptability, problem-solving ability, excellent computer skills, excellent written and verbal communication skills, the ability to multi-task as well as the ability to meet tight timelines.
